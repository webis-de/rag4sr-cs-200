Caption: Definitions and example adherences and violations for the five control criteria
| Control | Definition | Adherence | Violation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Matched on activities | Both conditions received the same activities, implemented in the same way OR both conditions did not receive any activities | IVR condition received a group pipetting task as did the conventional condition | IVR condition received a group pipetting task and the conventional condition received a solo pipetting task |
| Matched on non-IVR activities | The conventional condition received the same non-IVR activities as the IVR condition OR the IVR condition did not receive any non-IVR activities | The IVR condition received a paper-based worksheet as did the conventional condition | The IVR condition received a paper-based worksheet, but the conventional condition did not receive this worksheet activity |
| Matched on practice with dependent measure | Both conditions received practice with the dependent measure OR both conditions did not receive practice with the dependent measure | The IVR condition practiced retrieving information via multiple-choice questions as did the conventional condition | The IVR condition practiced retrieving information via multiple-choice questions but the conventional condition did not receive this practice |
| Matched on time spent learning the content | Both conditions received a lesson that was the same length (or within 20% difference) | The IVR condition lesson was 30 min in length as was the conventional condition lesson | The IVR condition lesson was 30 min in length but the conventional condition lesson was 15 min in length |
| Matched on the content | Both conditions received a lesson that involved the same topics, concepts, and/or procedures | The IVR condition lesson went into depth on five biology concepts as did the conventional condition | The IVR condition lesson went into depth on five biology concepts but the conventional condition only briefly covered these five concepts |
