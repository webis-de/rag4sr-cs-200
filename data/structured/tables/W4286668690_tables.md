Caption: Objectives of the studies where the extraction of gaze parameters is reported and the meaning of them concerning the usage into the sports science
| Author, Year | Captured Data | Study's objectives | Usage into sports |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Without considering sports-related purposes** | | | |
| Luidolt et al. (2020) [W3087755656] | Head motion, eye movements, pupil size | The creation of a VR/AR stimulation enabling perceptual realism comparable to this from RW. The HMDs cannot display the brightness and color as in RW. By creating visual effects within the VR scene, the authors tried to awaken a more realistic adapted impression for the users by developing a gaze-dependent stimulation that enabled physically plausible realistic lighting. | This method can enhance the realism of VR sports scenarios and, therefore, increase the VR immersion. The authors presented stepwise the algorithms realizing temporal eye adaptation, perceptual glare, visual acuity reduction, and scotopic color vision. Those can be further integrated into VR applications, and suggestions are made. |
| Kim & Kim (2020) [W3025244083] | Gaze fixations | Examining participants' visual perception processes during a wayfinding task. The analyses considered the clarity and recognition of spatial information during the controlled environment without disturbing external factors. | Spatial orientation is an essential coordination ability that allows athletes to specify their movements [W2776909509]. This study represents detecting the participants' attention by creating AOIs that capture the amount and the mean duration of gaze fixations. During sports-related research on motor performance, it has been found that picking up relevant visual information is required to complete various tasks [W2776909509]. This method can also be transferred during sports by analyzing athletes' gaze behavior to determine relevant visual cues. |
| Iskander et al. (2019) [W2953430552] | Vergence angle, IPD, POG | It was developed an assessment tool that enables interaction with virtual objects by using the eyes and reducing the VOC within head-mounted display VR. The goal is to examine visual fatigue during immersion in virtual environments caused by the VOC and recommend solutions for optimizing VR scenarios. | VOC is the main contributor to visual acuity and is essential for detecting depth cues. Using the biomechanical model could be an alternative to estimate eye behavior. The variability of vergence accommodation decreases with depth, so fixating nearer objects leads to increased fatigue. Using VR, for example, as learning sessions in which the users can observe athletes' or trainers' movements, distances with a specific length should be maintained to ensure less perceived fatigue. |
| Roth et al. (2017) [W2759603325] | Gaze accuracy and precision | Evaluation of a foveated rendering approach by recording eye tracking data during fixation tasks within different VR scenes and modes. The goal is to ensure low-latencies rendering, higher display refresh rates leading to high immersion, and less nausea. Foveated rendering depends on highly accurate ET data, and many approaches appear to improve them. | The foveated rendering could lead to higher immersion ensured through less nausea, coping high refresh rates, and providing higher realistic virtual environments without the need for expensive or unreachable technological equipment. Those facts are also crucial for sports-related since higher immersion leads to higher performance. A reduced inequality between both conditions could benefit a transfer in VR adapted skills to the real world. |
| Bozkir et al. 2021 [W3162947752] | Analyzing students' visual attention within a virtual classroom by letting them exploit object-of-interests. The study allows to imagine future realizable virtual training sessions and show some significant considerations of virtual learning space. | [W3162947752] gives insight into what a virtual learning session could be like in the future by using gaze data revealing users' visual attention within the virtual environment. Also, the interaction with a virtual instructor (different realistic-looking types) was examined using the gaze data extractable by the integrated ET system. Those analyses could be crucial for examining motor learning sessions within VR to determine the primary attentional cues, for example, during movement observation (self-motion and motion of opponents or teammates). | [W3162947752] gives insight into what a virtual learning session could be like in the future by using gaze data revealing users' visual attention within the virtual environment. Also, the interaction with a virtual instructor (different realistic-looking types) was examined using the gaze data extractable by the integrated ET system. Those analyses could be crucial for examining motor learning sessions within VR to determine the primary attentional cues, for example, during movement observation (self-motion and motion of opponents or teammates). |
| **Considering sports-related purposes** | | | |
| Mann et al. (2019) [W2997152647] | Saccades | The examination of the location of predictive eye-movements during a sport situation. The goal was to detect whether the participant's gaze was moving or fixed while hitting a bouncing tennis ball. | This study gives an impression of how the combined technic (HMD and ET) can be used for sports-related analyses. It shows that athletes seem to have predictive saccades direct the gaze above the location of the bouncing ball to gain information on ball tracking. To examine whether this also occurred in other ball sports, further investigations should be made. This could be important for creating a training scenario for novices since those locations allowing the extraction of valuable information can be highlighted within VR controlling participants' gaze for better learning adaptations. |
